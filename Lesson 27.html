The Writer's Dilemma

Read the editorial. Click highlighted terms to load their index card definition and hear pronunciation.

Truth be told, drafting a successful novel is an uphill battle. If you simply play it safe, your creative style will be conspicuous by its absence. To craft a masterpiece, you must learn to think outside the box, rather than trying to reinvent the wheel.

Of course, this is easier said than done when you are sick and tired of deadlines. If you take it for granted that publishers will accept your first draft, you will likely miss the boat.

At the end of the day, time will tell whether your book is a bitter pill to swallow or ends up selling like hotcakes. It is a matter of opinion which genres are best, but providing readers with rich food for thought is a guaranteed way to keep them hooked.

You might feel scared to death of failure when making your way in this industry. But if you keep making progress by leaps and bounds, you will eventually see the light at the end of the tunnel. Last but not least, keep your plots short and sweet, and make sure your messages are crystal clear!

Lexical Clichés

A cliché is an overused expression. In English, we try to avoid them in formal writing, but they are extremely common in conversational speech. Understanding them perfectly is essential to fluent comprehension and expression.
