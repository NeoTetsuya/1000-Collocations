The Symphony of Daily Life

Click high-contrast collocations to study their acoustic nuances, definitions, and pronunciations.

Yesterday morning, I woke up to the lovely sound of my wife playing the piano in the salon. In the next room, I could hear the soft sound of our children talking. But the peaceful atmosphere didn't last. Out in the alleyway, the fighting cats suddenly made horrible screeching sounds. "I can't hear you," I shouted into the phone, "there's a lot of background noise!" On top of that, my car was making an annoying buzzing noise whenever I turned on the air conditioner.

Living in the city is an acoustic adventure. Some signals are just a loud noise, but near the railway, it becomes a deafening sound/noise. Sometimes you only hear a faint sound of music, or a muffled sound through the thick building walls. There is a constant noise from the highway—a perpetual roar of traffic day and night. If you work in a factory, you live with the hum of machinery all day long. If you live next to college students, you hear their music blaring at all hours.

Yet nature is full of noises too. In the morning, you can enjoy birds chirping or hear dogs barking down the path. Near the coast, you can listen to the waves crashing, while the wind whistling through the trees creates a wild, remote feeling. When a storm arrives, the deep rumble of thunder rolls in, followed by a sudden thunderclap that makes everyone jump.

Eventually, we crave the absence of sound. Sometimes a room is strangely quiet—like when children are up to silent mischief. Other times, an awkward silence hangs in the air after an embarrassing joke, or someone lapses into a stony silence due to anger. There might even be a shocked silence after a sudden resignation. When the city streets become ominously quiet after midnight, we expect peace. But as silence descended over the empty streets, a screeching car alarm suddenly shatters the silence, breaking the illusion of an absolutely silent world.
