How do you make a decision? Do you make lists of pros and cons, advantages and disadvantages? If so, you're weighing the options — considering each possibility carefully before choosing.

Sometimes there are too many possibilities, and you may need to narrow down your choices to a shortlist. When the time comes to decide, you can make a choice, come to a decision, or reach a decision.

If you have difficulty deciding, you might want to sleep on it — think about it overnight. In formal language, we also say arrive at a decision. Once you've made up your mind, you may have a firm decision or an irrevocable decision.

If you're not sure, you may need to reconsider your decision. Another useful expression is to determine the best course of action. If you really can't decide, you can ask someone else to make the decision for you, leaving it up to them. People can also face a difficult decision, and sometimes the best thing is to go with your gut or trust your instincts. If you choose one option over another, you opt for something. When a decision is particularly important, you describe it as a major decision or a crucial decision.
