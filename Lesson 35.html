The Rules of Movement & Velocity

Read the verified lesson definitions. Click highlighted collocations to pull up speech guides and usage parameters.

Today you're going to learn the rules of movement and velocity! English has a number of words that mean "fast" — but each one has different collocations. Use fast to describe a vehicle, person, or action: a fast car, a fast train, or a fast runner, and actions like to work fast, drive fast, or learn fast.

Use quick to describe an event or action: a quick lunch, a quick glance, a quick shower, or a quick decision.

Use rapid to describe a change: a rapid increase/decline, a rapid change, rapid growth, or rapid progress.

The word speedy is often used with recovery and resolution: a speedy recovery from surgery, or a speedy resolution to the problem.

The word swift is often used with action, response, and reaction: The swift action of the police enabled the criminal to be caught immediately, or a swift response to our request.

The word prompt means "fast and punctual" — it is often used with information and payment or customer service. For example, you can give a prompt reply to an e-mail, or receive prompt delivery of a product.

The word hasty means "fast and in a rush" — He made a hasty exit from the party when he saw his ex-girlfriend walk in. This word often has a connotation of doing something without sufficient consideration. You might come to a hasty conclusion, or make a hasty decision that you'll regret later.

When you're driving, you need to pay attention to the speed limit. If you're driving at breakneck speed, you might need to slow down. If you get caught in a speed trap, you might have to pay a speeding ticket.

Of course, you might need to put on a burst of speed to pass a car that's moving at a snail's pace. If major construction is being done, traffic might slow to a crawl and eventually come to a standstill.

To talk about changes in speed, we can say something is gaining speed or speeding up (faster) or losing speed or slowing down (slower).

Finally, to talk about eating or walking in a slow and relaxed way, you can use the word "leisurely" — after a leisurely breakfast, we took a leisurely stroll through the park.

Velocity Physics

The vocabulary of speed and movement is remarkably structured. You describe a change as rapid but a recovery or resolution as speedy. On the other hand, you make a hasty decision if you act without thinking. Avoid mixing these native patterns.
