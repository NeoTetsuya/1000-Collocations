Lesson 20 Narrative

Explore town planning, skyscraper design, and urban landscape collocations. Click highlighted phrases to look up definitions.

When my parents retired last year, they traded the towering skyscrapers and noise pollution of Los Angeles for the peace and quiet of a small coastal city in southern France.

They said they were tired of living in a high-rise building right next to an industrial zone. Now they live in a cozy little house on the outskirts of the city.

Life in their new town is quite different from the hectic pace of L.A., where the bustling streets are strewn with litter and you can count on spending hours in traffic jams.

Instead, the narrow cobblestone streets of the city center are lined with eighteenth-century buildings that are now home to quaint shops and gourmet restaurants specializing in the local cuisine.

The best day to visit is Saturday, when there's an open-air market in the main square. You can buy souvenirs from the street vendors and have lunch at one of the trendy cafés in the area. There's virtually no street crime here, in contrast to the inner-city areas of L.A.

Is there anything that my parents miss about Los Angeles? Well, they do miss the cultural diversity. But they're past the age where being in a place with a pulsating nightlife is important to them.
