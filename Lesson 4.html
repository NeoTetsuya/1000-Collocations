Lesson 4 Narrative

Explore character and behavior collocations. Click any highlighted expression below to study its context.

Let's begin by looking at some collocations of words that describe character and behavior. An individual can have a strong personality or a painfully shy personality. Other aspects of your personality include being highly intelligent or having a razor-sharp mind. If you have a vivid imagination, you are creative.

People can be fiercely loyal to their friends. However, some people are set in their ways. It is better to be selfless and put others first.

Children often show their anger by throwing a temper tantrum.

If you are brutally honest, you tell the absolute truth even if it hurts, and you might hurt someone's feelings. If you make a mistake, you may have to swallow your pride and apologize.

It is fun to play a joke or play a prank on someone, but you shouldn't bear a grudge if they do the same to you. Don't act stupidly and make a fool of yourself.

Some people have a tendency to lose their temper.

A crisis will reveal your true character. In any situation, it's best to speak your mind.
