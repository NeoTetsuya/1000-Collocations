Lesson 2 Narrative

Explore relationships collocations. Click any highlighted expression below to study its context.

When you move to a new city, it can be hard to make friends. You'll probably have a number of casual acquaintances. Over time, some of these may become good friends or close friends.

Do you believe in love at first sight? That's when two people meet each other and immediately fall in love – perhaps because they have strong chemistry. Some people believe that their significant other is their soul mate.

It's possible for one person to have feelings for another person – but if the other person doesn't feel the same way, then the feeling is not mutual. The other person might say "he's/she's just not my type." Ah, there's nothing sadder than unrequited love!

Some people marry their childhood sweetheart or high school/college sweetheart. Other couples are introduced by a mutual friend. When the couple is ready to make a commitment, the man proposes to the woman. If the marriage is good, then we say the couple is happily married.

Unfortunately, not all love stories have a happy ending. Sometimes a husband or wife has an affair. We can also say that they are cheating on their husband/wife.
