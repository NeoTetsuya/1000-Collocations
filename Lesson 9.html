Lesson 9 Narrative

Explore musical collocations. Click any highlighted expression below to study its context.

My older brother Alex is very musically gifted. He can play any tune from memory, without looking at sheet music. He spends all his free time strumming a guitar, composing music, and writing song lyrics.

Alex and his friends formed a band that has attracted a sizable following in our city. They started out playing gigs in bars and clubs, and they used the money they earned to record their debut album.

A remixed track from that CD then became a massive hit on the local radio station thanks to its catchy tune and upbeat tempo.

Their second album will be released at the end of the year; they'll record it live at their next show. Alex dreams of going on tour - he's just waiting for his big break to get his foot in the door of the music industry.

As for me, I love singing along to my brother's music, but a career in music isn't for me - I'm completely tone-deaf and I always sing off-key!

Note: While I stick to simpler paths, Alex is completely deep into contemporary music and classical music.

He loves complex arrangements of orchestral music, whereas I tend to play heavy rock on repeat. Recently, he started preparing some solo albums alongside standard compilation albums to satisfy his passionate listeners who have formed a devoted cult following.
