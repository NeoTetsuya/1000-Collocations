The Spelunking Incident

Our exploration began late in the afternoon, just as the sun set and shadows lengthened across the valley. It was rapidly growing dark, forcing us to hasten our pace. To enter the forbidden cavern undetected, we had decided to move under cover of darkness.

As we stepped inside, the mouth of the cave was only dimly lit by the fading twilight. Soon, the path sloped downwards, and we were suddenly plunged into darkness. It was absolutely pitch dark—or as some Spelunkers prefer to say, pitch black. This was complete darkness; a thick, heavy, utter darkness that felt almost physical.

We switched on our headlamps, which cast erratic, flickering shadows against the limestone formations. I thought I saw a shadowy figure disappear into the deep shadows at the end of the corridor, but my companion claimed it was just my imagination. "Don't let dark thoughts or fear cloud your judgment," he whispered.

As we waited for our eyes to adapt, we spotted a faint outline of a passageway ahead. There was no shadow of a doubt that we had discovered the lost tomb. The walls were covered in ancient inscriptions shrouded in darkness for centuries. For the time being, we stood silently in the deepening gloom, enjoying our discovery.

Lexical Darkness

English maps descriptions of shadows and darkness to exact, fixed combinations. You move **under cover of darkness**, are **plunged into darkness**, or witness a **deepening gloom**. Avoid incorrect groupings like "shrouded by dark" or "thrown in dark" to maintain standard professional English.
