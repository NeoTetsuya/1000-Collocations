Lesson 25 Brief: Global Problems

Grounded in the official curriculum files. Click highlighted collocations to pull up policy intelligence files.

Today, humanity faces unprecedented challenges. More than one billion people are still trapped in extreme poverty, unable to secure basic necessities. In many developing regions, chronic food shortages lead to severe malnutrition, while millions lack access to safe drinking water. International organizations are desperately trying to eradicate poverty, but systemic barriers slow down their progress.

At the same time, we witness horrific human rights violations under dictatorial regimes. Children are forced into child labor in factories, and vulnerable demographics suffer from systemic sexual exploitation. Severe political instability has created millions of asylum seekers fleeing violence. In the worst scenarios, extreme regimes have resorted to ethnic cleansing, sparking devastating regional conflicts. To address these crisis zones, wealthier nations must step in and provide international aid, moving capital from donor countries to recipient countries.

Meanwhile, the ecological survival of our planet is in jeopardy due to rapid climate change. The burning of fossil fuels releases immense volumes of greenhouse gases, accelerating global warming and inducing rapid environmental degradation. We are continuously depleting natural resources, destroying the protective ozone layer, and polluting ecosystems with toxic waste. Accidental disasters like a major oil spill can wipe out coastal habitats instantly.

As severe natural disasters threaten the survival of endangered species, we must transition towards renewable energy and prioritize sustainable development to preserve our biosphere.
