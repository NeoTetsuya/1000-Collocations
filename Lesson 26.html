Lesson 26 Brief: Time

Grounded in the official curriculum files. Click highlighted collocations to pull up policy intelligence files.

If you have a lot of free time (or spare time), you can choose how to spend it. Some people like to spend time studying, while others prefer to waste time on social media—which is often a complete waste of time.

If your schedule is busy, you must try to make time or find time for exercise. Doing things efficiently will save time. If you have to wait at an airport, you can read a book to kill time or pass the time. When writing an exam, don't rush—you can take your time, but be careful not to run out of time before the instructor announces that your time is up.

To succeed, it is crucial to keep track of time. Always arrive on time for meetings, or at least in time to prepare your presentation. If you arrive at the last second, you are just in time. If you are running late, you are behind schedule, but if you plan in advance, you can finish ahead of time or right on schedule.

If you are short on time (or pressed for time), you might feel stressed. Everyone experiences a rough time (or hard time) occasionally, but at times it is best to simply take time off from work. Although we only go on vacation from time to time, it is always time well spent. For the time being, let's focus on mastering these collocations—you will learn them in no time!
