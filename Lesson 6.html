Lesson 6 Narrative

Explore domestic and housing collocations. Click any highlighted expression below to study its context.

I've always wanted to live in a place of my own. When I was in college, I shared a spacious living room with three roommates. It was a high-rise apartment in a run-down neighborhood. The rent was cheap, but we had to do the housework ourselves, and we argued about whose turn it was to take out the trash or make the bed.

In some cities, finding affordable housing is extremely difficult. However, after graduation, I got a job and moved in to a studio apartment in a nice residential area. It was fully furnished. It had a comfortable double bed, a small kitchen, and was only a short walk from a major road.

Before you move in, you usually have to sign a lease and pay a security deposit. You also have to pay the rent and the utilities. At the end of the lease, you can renew the lease or move out.

Now, I'm saving money to buy my dream home. I'd love to live in a newly built house, maybe with a small garden, in a quiet suburb. I don't want to live in a high-rise forever. I want to settle down, throw a housewarming party, and lead a quiet life.

Linguistic domestic Insight

Describing real estate and home chores in English relies on precise verb-noun groupings. Use collocations like "fully furnished" or "do the housework" instead of literal word-for-word translations.
